# CTF Writeups

This repository contains my personal CTF walkthroughs, notes, and learning writeups.

Most of these walkthroughs are created with the help of AI from notes I take while completing each room. The notes usually include commands I ran, outputs I found useful, mistakes I made, exploitation steps, privilege escalation paths, and anything important I learned along the way.

The AI is used to help turn those raw notes into a clearer, more readable walkthrough format. I still review and adjust the final writeups so they match what I actually did during the room.

## Purpose

The main goals of this repository are to:

* Document what I learned from each CTF room
* Keep a record of useful commands and techniques
* Make it easier to revisit rooms later
* Share beginner friendly walkthroughs with others
* Track my progress while improving my cybersecurity skills

## Writeup Style

I try to keep the walkthroughs practical and easy to follow.

Most writeups include:

* Enumeration steps
* Commands used during the room
* Why certain findings mattered
* Exploitation steps
* Privilege escalation steps
* Key lessons learned

Secrets such as flags, passwords, hashes, tokens, and private keys are usually masked or shortened where possible.

Example:

* THM{abc...xyz}
* Passw...123
* hash:first6...last6

## Important Note

These writeups are for learning and documentation purposes only.

The techniques shown here are intended for legal CTF environments such as TryHackMe, Hack The Box, and other authorised labs. Do not use these techniques against systems you do not own or do not have permission to test.
